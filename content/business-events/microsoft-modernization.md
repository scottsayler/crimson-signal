## Technology implications of Microsoft modernization

Microsoft modernization is rarely a licensing decision. It reshapes identity, security posture, collaboration patterns, and how technology is managed at every location.

### What changes

- **Identity becomes the perimeter** as applications move to cloud and hybrid models.
- **Security architecture** must unify across on-premises legacy and cloud-native services.
- **Collaboration patterns** shift, affecting how distributed teams and locations operate.
- **Licensing and cost models** change from capital expenditure to ongoing operational expense.

### Multi-location considerations

Rolling out Microsoft 365 or Azure across dozens or hundreds of locations requires more than a deployment script. Network readiness, local IT capability, and change management vary by site.

### Strategic questions

- Is your identity architecture ready for cloud-first operations?
- How will you phase migration across locations with different readiness levels?
- What is the total cost of ownership beyond licensing?
