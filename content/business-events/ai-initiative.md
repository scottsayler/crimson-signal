## Technology implications of AI adoption

AI initiatives succeed or fail on foundations that have nothing to do with models: data quality, governance, and operational readiness across every location.

### What changes

- **Data architecture** becomes a strategic asset, not a back-office function.
- **Governance requirements** escalate as AI touches customer-facing and regulated processes.
- **Infrastructure decisions** shift from periodic upgrades to continuous capacity planning.
- **Workforce implications** extend beyond IT to every role AI touches.

### The multi-location challenge

AI trained on data from one region may not perform at others. Location-specific regulations, languages, and operational patterns create complexity that centralized AI strategies overlook.

### Strategic questions

- Is your data ready for the use cases you are pursuing?
- Who owns AI governance across business units and locations?
- What is your realistic path from pilot to production?
