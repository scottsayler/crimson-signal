## Technology implications of security requirements

Security requirements in multi-location organizations are not solved by deploying more tools at headquarters. Every location is an endpoint, a data source, and a potential attack vector.

### What changes

- **Policy enforcement** must work consistently whether staff are at headquarters, a branch, or remote.
- **Network architecture** decisions directly affect security boundaries and monitoring capability.
- **Identity and access management** becomes the foundation for every other security control.
- **Compliance scope** expands with every location, partner integration, and data flow.

### Strategic questions

- Is your security architecture designed for distributed operations?
- Can you demonstrate compliance at every location, not just on paper?
- How do you balance security requirements with operational agility?
