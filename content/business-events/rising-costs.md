## Technology implications of rising costs

When technology costs rise faster than revenue, the problem is rarely a single vendor or contract. It is usually structural: sprawl, duplication, and decisions made without a location-aware view.

### What changes

- **Spend visibility** becomes a prerequisite for any meaningful optimization.
- **Contract and renewal cycles** create windows for consolidation or renegotiation.
- **Architecture decisions** made years ago may no longer match current business scale.
- **Shadow IT and SaaS sprawl** compound costs in ways centralized procurement cannot see.

### The multi-location multiplier

Per-location costs that seem manageable individually become significant at scale. Connectivity, support contracts, and redundant tooling across sites are common sources of hidden spend.

### Strategic questions

- Do you know what you are paying for at each location?
- Where is duplication creating unnecessary cost?
- What technology investments are delivering measurable business value?
