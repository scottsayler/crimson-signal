maturityObservations:
  - Groups with fewer than 15 locations rarely maintain a formal opening technology checklist — each opening team improvises.
  - Mature restaurant groups provision POS, networking, and guest-facing systems from a single blueprint per store format.
  - Franchise models with technology autonomy show higher per-location cost variance than corporate-owned rollouts.
  - PCI maturity correlates with enterprise payment environment visibility — not with the number of security tools deployed.
  - High-maturity groups measure opening readiness with a single facilities-and-IT milestone plan.
operationalPatterns:
  - Opening dates are set by real estate and labor timelines; technology becomes the constraint at handoff.
  - Menu and POS integration testing is compressed when opening timelines slip — revenue risk transfers to opening week.
  - Local carrier negotiations create connectivity inconsistency that operations attributes to staffing.
  - Back-of-house and guest-facing systems are provisioned on separate timelines without integrated readiness criteria.
  - Vendor contracts at individual locations constrain menu platform and payment decisions at the enterprise level.

## Context

Restaurant expansion technology is deceptively uniform — every location needs POS, payments, connectivity, and increasingly guest-facing digital touchpoints. The operational challenge is repeatability, not complexity.

These ranges apply most directly to groups opening three or more locations per year. Below that cadence, bespoke approaches remain viable without structural cost accumulation.
