maturityObservations:
  - Immature organizations cut technology spend by vendor category; mature organizations cut by business capability with explicit risk acceptance.
  - Cloud migration is frequently positioned as a cost solution before workload-level economics are analyzed.
  - Organizations with centralized contract visibility rationalize faster than those with regional procurement autonomy.
  - PCI scope management maturity is a leading indicator of whether cost cuts will create compliance exposure.
  - High-maturity organizations document accepted risk when security or continuity spending is reduced.
operationalPatterns:
  - Cost mandates arrive as percentage targets before spend is mapped to capabilities.
  - Store closure decisions exclude technology exit costs from real estate economics.
  - Vendor consolidation pursued for contract savings increases integration complexity.
  - Per-department collaboration tool cuts recreate the duplication enterprise standardization was meant to eliminate.
  - Disaster recovery capabilities are reduced without explicit executive risk sign-off.

## Context

Retail cost pressure follows a predictable pattern: mandates precede visibility. These ranges help leadership teams calibrate expectations before rationalization conversations begin.

Directional benchmarks are most useful when paired with a capability map — without one, organizations typically cycle through two rounds of cuts before reaching defensible decisions.
