## Technology context for restaurants

Restaurant organizations operate at the intersection of guest experience and operational precision. Technology failures in kitchen systems, POS, or connectivity are immediately visible to customers and staff.

### Location complexity

A restaurant footprint may include company-owned locations, franchises, ghost kitchens, and multi-brand concepts — each with different technology ownership models and brand standards.

### Common technology pressures

- **Kitchen and POS integration** — order accuracy depends on synchronized front-of-house and back-of-house systems.
- **Guest connectivity and ordering** — Wi-Fi, online ordering, and delivery platforms affect reviews and repeat visits.
- **Menu and pricing consistency** — loyalty programs and promotional rules must work identically across locations.
- **Labor and compliance** — scheduling, tip pooling, and payroll systems must be live before operational scale.

### How business events affect restaurants

Opening new locations, customer complaints, and outages are the most frequent triggers for technology strategy conversations in restaurants.
