## Technology context for healthcare

Healthcare organizations manage technology across hospitals, clinics, labs, and administrative offices — each with distinct compliance requirements, clinical workflows, and patient experience demands.

### Regulatory landscape

HIPAA, state privacy laws, and clinical accreditation standards create technology requirements that vary by care setting and data type. Security is not optional — it is a license to operate.

### Common technology pressures

- **EHR integration** across acquired practices and new locations.
- **Telehealth infrastructure** that must work reliably for clinicians and patients.
- **Medical device connectivity** on networks shared with administrative systems.
- **Workforce collaboration** across clinical and non-clinical teams.

### How business events affect healthcare

Acquisitions, security requirements, and Microsoft modernization are particularly acute in healthcare, where technology decisions have direct patient safety and compliance implications.
