## Technology context for retail

Retail organizations operate at the intersection of physical and digital customer experience. Technology decisions affect every transaction, every interaction, and every location's ability to compete.

### Location complexity

A retail footprint may include flagship stores, mall locations, strip centers, and pop-ups — each with different connectivity constraints, landlord requirements, and customer expectations.

### Common technology pressures

- **Omnichannel consistency** — customers expect the same experience online and in-store.
- **POS and payment reliability** — downtime directly translates to lost revenue.
- **Inventory visibility** — real-time data across locations drives fulfillment and customer satisfaction.
- **Seasonal scaling** — technology must handle peak demand without over-provisioning year-round.

### How business events affect retail

Opening new stores, rising costs, and customer complaints are the most frequent triggers for technology strategy conversations in retail. Each event has distinct implications depending on store format, geography, and brand standards.
