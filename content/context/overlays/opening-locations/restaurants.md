  - Kitchen display, POS, and payment systems must be synchronized before the first service — partial deployments create order errors and guest complaints.
  - Franchise and multi-brand models introduce technology variation that conflicts with menu, pricing, and loyalty program consistency.
  - Guest Wi-Fi, online ordering, and delivery platform integrations are often treated as add-ons rather than opening-day requirements.
blind_spots:
  - Back-of-house networking is frequently under-scoped compared to front-of-house POS, leaving kitchen systems on fragile connectivity.
  - Labor scheduling and tip pooling systems are often provisioned after POS go-live, creating payroll and compliance gaps in early weeks.
questions_to_explore:
  - Can this location run a full service — dine-in, takeout, and delivery — on opening day without manual workarounds?
  - Which menu, pricing, and loyalty configurations must be identical to existing locations versus locally adapted?
